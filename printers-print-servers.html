🖨️ Printers & Print Servers

Printers

Connect to the office print server, fix stuck print queues, and manage installed printers.

Connect to Print Server

#What to DoHow to Do It
1Find your office printerAsk your IT team for the name of the print server and the printer.
2Connect to print serverOpen File Explorer, type \\printserver in the address bar (IT will give you the exact name). Double-click your printer to install it.

Fix Stuck Print Queue

#What to DoHow to Do It
1Cancel stuck jobs (UI)Go to Settings > Printers, click your printer, open the print queue, right-click stuck jobs and click Cancel.
2Clear queue via PowerShellUse the one-liner below to stop the spooler, delete stuck jobs, and restart.

Manage Printers

#What to DoHow to Do It
1Check correct printer before printingAlways verify the printer name shown in the print dialog before clicking Print.
2Update printer driverGo to Device Manager > Printers > right-click your printer > Update Driver > Search Automatically.
3Remove old printersGo to Settings > Bluetooth & devices > Printers & scanners, click a printer you no longer use, and click Remove Device.

Quick Reference — One-Liners

TaskCommand (PowerShell — Run as Admin)
Clear stuck print queueStop-Service Spooler -Force; Remove-Item -Path "C:\Windows\System32\spool\PRINTERS\*" -Force -ErrorAction SilentlyContinue; Start-Service Spooler
List installed printersGet-Printer | Select-Object Name, PortName, PrinterStatus, Default
Add network printerAdd-Printer -ConnectionName "\\printserver\PrinterName"
Remove a printerRemove-Printer -Name "PrinterName"
Set default printerSet-Printer -Name "PrinterName" -Shared $false; (New-Object -ComObject WScript.Network).SetDefaultPrinter("PrinterName")
Restart print spoolerRestart-Service Spooler -Force
