🖨️ Printer Setup & Troubleshooting

Printers

Add local and network printers, fix stuck queues, update drivers, and remove old printers.

Add a Printer

#What to DoHow to Do It
1Add a local printerGo to Settings > Bluetooth & devices > Printers & scanners > Add device.
2Add a network printerOpen File Explorer, type \\printserver in the address bar. Double-click your printer to install it.

Fix Stuck Print Queue

#What to DoHow to Do It
1Cancel stuck jobs (UI)Go to Settings > Printers, click your printer, open the print queue, right-click stuck jobs and click Cancel.
2Clear queue via PowerShellUse the one-liner below to stop the spooler, wipe stuck jobs, and restart.

Manage & Troubleshoot Printers

#What to DoHow to Do It
1Wrong printer prints jobBefore printing, always check the printer name shown in the print dialog. Change it if needed.
2Update printer driverGo to Device Manager > Printers > right-click your printer > Update Driver > Search Automatically.
3Remove old printersGo to Settings > Bluetooth & devices > Printers & scanners, click the old printer, and click Remove Device.

Quick Reference — One-Liners

TaskCommand (PowerShell — Run as Admin)
Clear stuck print queueStop-Service Spooler -Force; Remove-Item -Path "C:\Windows\System32\spool\PRINTERS\*" -Force -ErrorAction SilentlyContinue; Start-Service Spooler
List installed printersGet-Printer | Select-Object Name, PortName, PrinterStatus, Default
Add network printerAdd-Printer -ConnectionName "\\printserver\PrinterName"
Remove a printerRemove-Printer -Name "PrinterName"
Restart print spoolerRestart-Service Spooler -Force
