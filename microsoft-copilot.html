🤖 Microsoft Copilot

Copilot

Use Copilot built into Microsoft 365 apps — Word, Outlook, Teams, and Excel.

Copilot in Word

#What to DoHow to Do It
1Open Copilot in WordOpen a Word document. Click the Copilot icon in the ribbon or press Alt+I.
2Draft contentClick Draft with Copilot, describe what you want written, and click Generate.
3Summarize a documentClick the Copilot icon > Summarize. Copilot will generate a short summary of the document.
4Rewrite or improve textSelect text, right-click > Rewrite with Copilot, or use the Copilot pane to request changes.

Copilot in Outlook

#What to DoHow to Do It
1Draft an emailClick New Email > Copilot icon > Draft with Copilot. Describe what you want and click Generate.
2Summarize an email threadOpen the email thread. Click the Copilot icon at the top > Summarize.
3Adjust tone or lengthAfter Copilot generates a draft, click Regenerate and specify: Make it shorter, more formal, etc.

Copilot in Teams

#What to DoHow to Do It
1Summarize a meetingDuring or after a meeting, click the Copilot icon in the meeting toolbar. Select Recap or ask "What did I miss?"
2Summarize a chat threadIn a Teams chat, click the Copilot icon (✨) at the top right of the chat > Summarize.
3Ask questions about a meetingIn the Copilot pane during a meeting, type a question like "What action items were mentioned?"

Copilot in Excel

#What to DoHow to Do It
1Analyze data with CopilotSelect a data range or table. Click Copilot in the ribbon. Ask it to "Analyze this data" or "Show trends."
2Create a chartWith data selected, open Copilot and ask "Create a bar chart of sales by month."
3Add a formulaClick a cell, open Copilot, and ask "Write a formula to sum column B where column A equals Q1."

Copilot Privacy & Data Policy

#RuleDetail
1Follow company data policyCopilot follows your organization's Microsoft 365 data governance settings. Your prompts stay within the tenant.
2Do not enter confidential dataAvoid entering PII, patient data, or trade secrets into Copilot prompts unless your org has approved the use case.
3Verify AI outputAlways review Copilot-generated content before sending or publishing — it can make mistakes.
